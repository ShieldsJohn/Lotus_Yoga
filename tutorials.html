Beginner

Hero pose (Virasana)

• Start by kneeling on the floor with your knees together and your feet slightly wider than hip-width apart. • Point your toes backward and sit on your heels. • Keep your spine straight and shoulders relaxed. • You can place your hands on your thighs or in your lap. • Hold the pose for a few breaths, gradually increasing the time as you become more comfortable.

Child's pose (Balasana)

• Begin on your hands and knees in a tabletop position. • Sit back onto your heels, keeping your big toes together and knees apart. • Extend your arms forward on the mat and lower your forehead to the ground. • Relax your entire body, focusing on deep breaths.

Extended puppy pose (Uttana Shishosana)

• Start in a tabletop position on your hands and knees. • Walk your hands forward while lowering your chest toward the mat. • Keep your hips stacked over your knees. • Stretch your arms and lower your forehead to the ground. • Lengthen your spine, feeling a stretch in your upper body.

Intermediate

Bridge pose (Setu Bandhasana)

• Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet hip-width apart, flat on the floor. • Place your arms alongside your body, palms facing down. • Press through your feet and lift your hips off the ground. • Roll your shoulders underneath you and clasp your hands together if possible. • Keep your thighs parallel to each other and engage your glutes. • Hold for a few breaths and then gently release.

Downward-facing dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

• Start in a tabletop position with your wrists under your shoulders and knees under your hips. • Tuck your toes under, exhale, and lift your hips toward the ceiling. • Straighten your legs as much as your flexibility allows. • Spread your fingers wide and press your palms into the mat. • Relax your neck and let your head hang between your arms. • Press your heels down toward the floor.

Low lunge (Anjaneyasana)

• Begin in a downward-facing dog position. • Step your right foot forward between your hands. • Lower your left knee to the ground and untuck your toes. • Keep your right knee directly above your right ankle. • Inhale and lift your chest, arching your back slightly. • You can place your hands on your right thigh or reach them overhead for a deeper stretch. • Hold for a few breaths and then switch to the other side.

Advanced

Bow pose from on all fours (Dhanurasana)

• Start in a tabletop position on your hands and knees. • Reach your right arm forward and your left leg backward. • Simultaneously, lift your right arm and left leg off the ground, balancing on your remaining hand and knee. • Engage your core and arch your back, resembling a bow shape. • Hold briefly and then return to the tabletop position. • Repeat on the other side.

Extended side-angle pose (Utthita Parsvakonasana)

• Begin in a wide-legged stance with your feet about 3-4 feet apart. • Turn your right foot out 90 degrees and bend your right knee, aligning it with your ankle. • Extend your arms, reaching your right hand toward the ground and your left hand overhead. • Keep your torso lengthened and your chest open. • Gaze up at your left hand if comfortable. • Hold for a few breaths and then switch to the other side.

Mermaid pose (Eka Pada Rajakapotasana)

• Start in a seated position with your legs extended. • Bend your right knee and bring your heel close to your right hip. • Cross your right foot over your left thigh. • Bend your left knee and bring your left foot close to your left hip. • Gently lower your upper body backward and use your hands for support. • You can reach your right arm behind your back to hold onto your right foot if flexibility allows. • Keep your chest open and breathe deeply. • Repeat on the other side.
